⚠ Root cause of the ~358 "never really cancelled" bucket: the 31 May 2025 shopify-consumer incident (347 of these orders)

Confirmed in git: hotfix 0e1e730eb (2025-05-31 17:13 IST, "removed SlackUtils calls in shopify-consumer") ripped the Slack alerting out of cancel-order-app.ts / sync-order-app.ts. The alerts were wrapped in backOff retry (3 attempts, exponential), so a failing Slack webhook stalled and failed SQS message processing, and messages were redelivered. This coincided with a manual Shopify bulk-cancel that over-selected ("selected the odd 400... it canceled a few more, some bug" - Engineering chat, ~18:02). The result was a mass cancel + restock + payment-void sweep of orders that had already been delivered months earlier.

Interpretation: these are incident artifacts, not real customer cancellations. The goods were delivered and (for COD) cash was collected by couriers back in Jan-May; the cancel/restock/void happened later during the incident. The exposure is accounting-integrity, not customer liability: phantom restocks (inventory overstated for delivered units) and unrecognized / unmatched COD revenue. Correct action is to reverse the phantom restocks and re-recognize the revenue - not to investigate each as a dispute. These rows are excluded from the 28 exploit cases (none of the exploit refunds fall on 31 May).

⚠ Re-verification: the 28 candidates collapse to 5 confirmed exploits once refund timing is checked

The 28 were flagged on "full SUCCESS refund AND courier-verified delivery" - both true, but the original pass never checked the order of those two events. A real exploit needs the goods delivered first, then a customer self-refund. Comparing each refund's exact timestamp against its delivery scan (and the refund-admin / cancellation tables) reclassifies all 28:

5 · ₹17,875
CONFIRMED EXPLOIT - delivered, THEN a customer self-cancel auto-refund. Axita, Vivek, Pooja, Priyank, Milind (Milind refunded 15 min after delivery)
21 · ₹84,829
OPERATIONAL LEAK - refund landed 0.4-4.7 days BEFORE delivery; parcel already manifested, cancelled+refunded in transit, delivered anyway, returnedQty 0. A logistics/recovery problem, not deliberate abuse
2 · ₹6,413
CS REFUND - admin-issued post-delivery by Rajesh ("received only the outer box", "kettle too big to use"). Legitimate service refunds

Khushi Gehlot is not a confirmed repeat exploiter. Her 3 orders are all operational leaks - each refund preceded delivery. They are instead soft-exploit flagged: cancelled citing "delivery too long" with goods never returned, a plausible but unprovable "claim-delay-then-keep" pattern (11 such cases, ₹37,151, incl. the Rajdeep Sarkar internal ₹60 test). Separating a genuine logistics leak from soft-exploit needs RTO / courier-POD / customer-contact evidence that timestamps alone cannot give.

Enabling bug (unchanged): the refund flow never checks sibling suborders' fulfillment state, and the "order is shipped, cannot cancel" guard in cancel-order-app has been dead since Jan 2025 (misspelled field order_fullfilment + mapping lookup that misses split orders). It lets both the exploits and the leaks through.
