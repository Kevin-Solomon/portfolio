How to read the three categories - each refund is classified by when it happened relative to courier delivery, plus who issued it:
  • Hard exploit (a.k.a. "confirmed exploit"): goods delivered first, then the customer self-cancels and keeps them. The timeline proves intent - they had the goods in hand when they triggered the refund. The 5 ours-only cases.
  • Ops leak: the customer cancelled before delivery (parcel still in transit), got refunded, and the parcel leaked out and was delivered anyway. On its face this is a logistics failure, not the customer's doing - they didn't have the goods when they cancelled.
  • Soft exploit ⚠: an ops-leak case with suspicious characteristics (cancelled citing "delivery too long", goods never returned, repeat pattern) suggesting the customer may have gamed it deliberately - just in a way the data can't confirm. Needs RTO / courier-POD / customer-contact evidence to prove or clear.
